Trigonometric Functions

Welcome to the Trigonometric Functions section. In this lesson, you will explore the fundamental trigonometric functions, their applications, and their representations on the unit circle. By understanding these concepts, you'll gain valuable insights into how angles and sides relate in trigonometry, as well as how to solve various real-world problems using these functions. Definition of Trigonometric Functions

The main trigonometric functions are sine, cosine, and tangent, defined based on the sides of a right-angled triangle.

  • Sine (sin): Opposite side / Hypotenuse.
  • Cosine (cos): Adjacent side / Hypotenuse.
  • Tangent (tan): Opposite side / Adjacent side.

Reciprocal Trigonometric Functions

  • Secant (sec): Reciprocal of cosine.
  • Cosecant (csc): Reciprocal of sine.
  • Cotangent (cot): Reciprocal of tangent.

Graphs of Trigonometric Functions

Trigonometric functions are periodic and have specific shapes. The sine and cosine graphs oscillate between -1 and 1, while the tangent graph has vertical asymptotes at certain points.
