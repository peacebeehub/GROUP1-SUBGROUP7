Trigonometric Identities

Welcome to the world of Trigonometric Identities! These are fundamental tools used to simplify and solve complex trigonometric expressions. Mastering these identities will not only make problem-solving easier but will also give you a deeper understanding of trigonometric functions and their relationships. Let's dive in and explore the most essential identities in trigonometry!

What are Trigonometric Identities?

Trigonometric identities are equations that involve trigonometric functions and are true for every value of the variables involved. They provide useful relationships between different trigonometric functions and help in simplifying and solving complex trigonometric equations.

Co-Function Identities

Co-function identities relate the trigonometric functions of complementary angles (angles that add up to \( 90^\circ \) or \( \frac{\pi}{2} \)). For example:

  • Co-function: \( \sin(90^\circ - \theta) = \cos \theta \)

 

Sum and Difference Identities

These identities allow us to find the trigonometric function of an angle that is a sum or difference of two known angles. Example:

  • \( \sin(A \pm B) = \sin A \cos B \pm \cos A \sin B \)

Double Angle Identities

Double angle identities are used to express trigonometric functions of double angles in terms of single angles:

  • \( \sin 2\theta = 2\sin \theta \cos \theta \)
